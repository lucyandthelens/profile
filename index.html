My journey into photography started at a very young age from the moment I picked up a disposable camera at the age of 8 my passion ignited. I am mostly a self-taught photographer. The majority of my photographic work was taken on a compact camera until, I was gifted a DSLR camera for Christmas in 2014 which enabled me to take higher quality photos thus fuelling my enthusiasm for photography. Shortly after that at the start of 2015 I did a ten-week intro to photography course to develop my skills. I was able to enhance my skills through reading a variety of photography books such as read this if you want to take great photographs which provided me with a more in depth look into portrait photography and photography beyond auto by Chris Gatcum which gave me a deeper understanding of manual photo techniques.

Since joining Preston photographic society in 2015 I have worked with a variety of models, including solo shoots and workshops. I also enter monthly competitions and participate in photo walks. In the 2016/17 season I finished third overall in the novice category only one point behind the winner.
